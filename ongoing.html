Post-doc | UC Davis Center for Population Biology

Genetic paths to evolutionary rescue and the distribution of fitness effects along them.
With Guillaume Martin and Sally Otto.
We're using branching process models to calculate the probability of rescue by an arbitrary number of mutational steps and looking at the growth rates of each genotype along these genetic paths to rescue. poster

Genetic signatures of evolutionary rescue.
With Graham Coop.
We're modeling evolutionary rescue (by a single selective sweep) forward in time using branching processes and then putting the dynamics into a coalescent framework to observe the patterns evolutionary rescue is expected to leave around the selected site (e.g., dips in heterozygosity). talk

During environmental change, cooperation can promote rescue or lead to evolutionary suicide.
With Gil Henriques and Michael Doebeli.
We're using adaptive dynamics to see how investment in public goods affects, and is affected by, adaptation to environmental change.

Evolution affects fisheries projections under climate change.
With Joey Bernhardt and William Cheung.
We're using metabolic theory and evolving integral projection models to predict how fish body size distributions and population growth rates are expected to change with warming oceans.

Evolutionary tipping points.
With Ophélie Ronce.
We're investigating when and why evolutionary tipping points arise in stage-structured models, and where else they might appear.
